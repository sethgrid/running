300 Days of Run Rules & FAQ

What are the rules?
  1. Run a minimum of 300 Days in 2016
    • All runs have to occur between January 1, 2016 and December 31, 2016
    • Runs have to be a minimum of 1 mile in length
    • Run speed has to be at least 4 miles per hour (15 minute miles)
    • Runs can be on a treadmill
  2. Commit to raise money for a charity of your choice
    • You decide on your charity of choice
    • You set your own goal for fundraising
  3. Run (a lot!) and have fun!
How much does it cost to participate?
Absolutely nothing! We do ask though that you commit to raise money for a charity of your choice.
Why do I have to commit to raise money for a charity?
In lieu of an entry fee into the event, we ask that you commit to raise some amount of money for a charity of YOUR choice. Part of the objective of 300 Days of Run is to make an impact on the world beyond your exceptional achievement.
How much should I raise for my charity?
You get to determine that. BE BOLD! We’ve partnered with CrowdRise to support your fundraising endeavor to be able to reach out to your friends, family and the world to create good and donations. Maybe you set $300, $1 for each day you run or why not $3000! It’s totally up to you. You know that you are going to be talking about this to all of your friends, or maybe not, but still it needs to be more than just about you! Seriously though, what better way to celebrate this milestone achievement than helping to support some real people in need.

Who can participate?
Anyone. You will need to be able to track your runs and make the minimum run distance and speed for the run. While 300 days of running in a year may seem outrageous, it is an achievable goal for those who are determined to make it happen.
Do I have to be a pro athlete to do this?
Nope. You just need to be determined to get out and run at least 300 days in 2016. Actually, since the minimum run is only 1 miles, this "endurance running" event is accessible to a wide range of runners from casual to professional.
Why should I participate?
Besides achieving an incredible running feat, you will be raising money for charity and helping to make positive change in the world. Running to help change the world – is there anything better than that?
What items will you track?
We will be tracking: Date of Run, Distance, Time, Pace, Elevation Gain and Money Raised.
Who is organizing this challenge?
300 Days of Run is organized by 300 Days Inc, a Colorado not-for-profit organization in application for 501(c)3 tax-exempt status.
What is Strava?
Strava lets you track your runs via your iPhone, Android device or dedicated GPS device and helps you analyze and quantify your performance. For more information on Strava visit https://www.strava.com/features. If you do not have an Strava account, you can set one up for FREE at Strava.com
What is Crowdrise?
CrowdRise is a fundraising site for charitable and personal causes. You are prompted to set up your CrowdRise fundraising page as part of the 300 Days of Run event when you register. There is no charge for this and is totally free as part of the challenge!
What are the Monthly Challenges?
Each month throughout the year, there will be a special challenge (in addition to the already awesome 300 Days). For example, Running all 29 days in February… it’s a once in every 4 year opportunity and we’ll have a special prize to go along with it… More information on the monthly challenges coming soon.
Is there a shirt and medal for finishing?
We will have special gear and ceremonial 300 Day memento available for purchase for qualifying individuals. There will also be prizes during the year for challenge winners as they occur.
Where did the 300 Days of Run come from?
It came one day while running out on a trail. Trying to figure out the ‘next’ goal for a few people who liked to create ‘crazy’ running goals for common runners, 300 Days of Run was born. The idea was also to make it something bigger than just the runner and his or her personal running goal. We wanted to be able make the milestone MORE memorable by joining it with the satisfaction of raising money for charity at the same time in order to make the achievement even more outstanding.
Do you have a flyer so I can share 300 Days of Run with my friends or in my store?
Yes we do! Download this PDF and share the pull tags with your friends. The flyer is an ready to print (1 color) and post anywhere you want - bring one to your favorite running store or club and let them know you’re running 300 Days in 2016!
Does 2016 being a leap year help me in any way?
Yes! It allows you to miss 66 days of running over the year instead of the usual 65. Bonus!
Should I consult a doctor before participating?
Yes. We also recommend you get your head examined because this thing you are about to do is crazy!
Is this really a smart thing to do?
Probably not. We like to think there is stupid (small s) and Stupid (Big S), and as long as you keep it in the small s range you're golden.
What's the latest I can start and still complete 300 days of running in 2016?
March 7, 2016 and then you'll need to run everyday through the year end.
When is the soonest I can finish 300 days of running in 2016?
If you run continuous days starting January 2016, you will run your 300th run on Wednesday October 26, 2016.
How do I remove my name from the participant list?
If you would like to remove yourself from 300 Days of Run, please send an email to remove@300daysofrun.com and we'll take care of it.
